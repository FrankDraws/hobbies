Hobbies for Learning

✦

What would you do even if no one was watching? — The first question worth answering

How to Choose

Ask yourself:

  • What am I genuinely curious about? (Not what sounds cool.)
  • What would I do even if no one was watching?
  • What skill would make me feel more competent?
  • What would I love to teach someone else?

You don't need six hobbies. You need one that genuinely excites you and the commitment to actually do it. Learn. And share it.

Source: Hobbies on Substack

I

Hobbies That Make You Smarter

  1. Research as a hobby – Pick topics you're curious about & go deep using YouTube courses, research papers, podcasts, books
  2. Learn a new language – Spanish, French, Mandarin, sign language
  3. Study personal finance – Budgeting, investing, understanding money
  4. Learn data analysis – Excel, Google Sheets, basic statistics
  5. Study logic and critical thinking – Reasoning, fallacies, argumentation
  6. Learn vibe coding – Use AI to help you build things without traditional coding
  7. Master public speaking – Toastmasters, online courses, practice presentations
  8. Learn chess – Strategy, pattern recognition, competitive thinking
  9. Study etymology – Origins of words, language evolution
  10. Learn debate – Argumentation, persuasion, structured thinking
  11. Study art history – Movements, context, cultural analysis
  12. Learn music theory – Understanding composition, scales, harmony
  13. Study astronomy – Stars, planets, space exploration
  14. Learn speed reading – Information processing, comprehension techniques
  15. Master memory techniques – Mnemonics, spatial memory, recall systems

IV

Hobbies That Build Community

  1. Host a book club – Curate selections, lead discussions, build connection
  2. Host dinner parties – Cooking for friends, creating experiences
  3. Mentoring – Guide someone in your field or interest area
  4. Teaching workshops – Share your skills in group settings
  5. Volunteering – Causes you care about, structured giving back
  6. Community organizing – Neighborhood events, local initiatives
  7. Join or start a Discord / Slack – Build online community around shared interests
  8. Organize meetups – Networking events, hobby groups, social gatherings
  9. Start a study group – Collective learning, accountability, discussion
  10. Community gardening – Shared plots, urban farming, neighborhood beautification
